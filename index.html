DIY Science Experiments

Baking Soda Volcano

Create a classic erupting volcano using simple household ingredients.

Watch Baking soda volcano Tutorial Video

Materials:

  • Baking soda
  • Vinegar
  • Dish soap
  • Red food coloring
  • Plastic bottle
  • Clay or playdough

Instructions:

  1. Shape the clay around the bottle to form a volcano.
  2. Add baking soda, dish soap, and food coloring to the bottle.
  3. Pour in vinegar and watch the eruption!

Rainbow in a Jar

Learn about density by layering colored liquids to create a rainbow effect.

Watch Rainbow in a Jar Tutorial Video

Materials:

  • Honey
  • Dish soap
  • Water
  • Vegetable oil
  • Rubbing alcohol
  • Food coloring
  • Clear glass jar

Instructions:

  1. Color each liquid with different food coloring.
  2. Slowly pour each liquid into the jar in order of density.
  3. Observe the layers forming a rainbow.

Static Electricity Butterfly

Explore static electricity by making a paper butterfly flap its wings.

Watch Static Electricity Butterfly Tutorial Video

Materials:

  • Lightweight paper
  • Balloon
  • Scissors
  • Tape
  • String

Instructions:

  1. Cut out a butterfly shape from the paper.
  2. Attach the butterfly to a string and tape it to a surface.
  3. Rub the balloon on your hair to create static.
  4. Bring the balloon close to the butterfly and watch the wings move.

Homemade Lava Lamp

Create a colorful lava lamp using oil, water, and effervescent tablets.

Watch Homemade Lava Lamp Tutorial Video

Materials:

  • Clear plastic bottle or glass jar
  • Vegetable oil
  • Water
  • Food coloring
  • Alka-Seltzer tablets

Instructions:

  1. Fill the bottle mostly with oil, then add water to fill the rest.
  2. Add a few drops of food coloring.
  3. Break an Alka-Seltzer tablet into pieces and drop them in one at a time.
  4. Watch the colorful bubbles rise and fall like a lava lamp.

Walking Water

Observe capillary action by moving colored water between cups using paper towels.

Watch Walking Water Tutorial Video

Materials:

  • 3 clear cups
  • Paper towels
  • Water
  • Food coloring (red, blue, yellow)

Instructions:

  1. Fill the first and third cups with colored water (red and blue).
  2. Place empty cup in the middle.
  3. Fold paper towels into strips and place one end in colored water and the other in the empty cup.
  4. Watch as water "walks" through the paper towels and mixes colors.
